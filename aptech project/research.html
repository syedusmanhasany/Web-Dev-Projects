New Researches in Dentistry

Study finds link between gum disease and heart disease

A new study has found that people with gum disease are at a higher risk of developing heart disease. The study, which followed over 10,000 people for 10 years, found that those with gum disease were 50% more likely to develop heart disease than those without gum disease.

Regular dental check-ups and cleanings can help prevent gum disease. People with heart disease should pay extra attention to their oral health.

Study shows dental anxiety can be treated with cognitive behavioral therapy

A new study has found that cognitive behavioral therapy (CBT) can be an effective treatment for dental anxiety. The study followed 100 people with dental anxiety and found that those who received CBT had a significant reduction in their anxiety levels.

CBT is a form of talk therapy that focuses on changing negative thoughts and behaviors. Other treatments for dental anxiety include sedation and relaxation techniques.
